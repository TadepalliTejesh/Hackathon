Let's Get Started

What is PACE?

The Plankton, Aerosol, Cloud, ocean Ecosystem (PACE) mission is helping scientists study the Earth's oceans, atmosphere, and their impact on climate. With data on ocean color, aerosols, and clouds, PACE provides crucial insights into climate change and environmental health.

Our platform brings this fascinating science into classrooms, helping students of all ages explore the wonders of Earth science.

Why Do we need PACE?

PACE's data will help us better understand how the ocean and atmosphere exchange carbon dioxide. In addition, it will reveal how aerosols might fuel phytoplankton growth in the surface ocean. Novel uses of PACE data will benefit our economy and society. For example, it will help identify the extent and duration of harmful algal blooms. PACE will extend and expand NASA's long-term observations of our living planet. By doing so, it will take Earth's pulse in new ways for decades to come.

PACE-Objectives

  • Extend key systematic ocean biological, ecological, and biogeochemical climate data records and cloud and aerosol climate records.
  • Make new global measurements of ocean color to improve our understanding of the carbon cycle and ocean ecosystem responses to a changing climate.
  • Collect global observations of aerosol and cloud properties, focusing on reducing the largest uncertainties in climate and radiative forcing models of the Earth system.
  • Improve our understanding of how aerosols influence ocean biogeochemical cycles and ecosystems and how ocean biological and photochemical processes affect the atmosphere.

Discoveries of PACE

  • PACE's Ocean Color Instrument (OCI) can differentiate between different communities of phytoplankton in the ocean. This information can help scientists study microscopic life in the ocean and fisheries health

  • PACE's polarimeters, SPEXone and HARP2, can measure the height of aerosols and smoke plumes. This information can help scientists improve their understanding of how aerosols and clouds interact with each other and the climate.

  • PACE's data can help scientists track and study the fine particulate matter that is emitted into the atmosphere when wildfires burn.

  • PACE's data can help scientists identify the extent and duration of harmful algal blooms and help scientists better understand how climate change is affecting ocean phytoplankton blooms.

  • PACE's data can help scientists understand how particles in the air, like dust and smoke, can impact air quality.
